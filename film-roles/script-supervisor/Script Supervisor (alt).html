What Is a Script Supervisor?

A Script Supervisor (also called Script Continuity Supervisor) is the film set's guardian of consistency. They ensure that every detail matches perfectly between shots, scenes, and takes.

Track continuity of costumes, props, and actor positions
Time scenes and maintain script notes
Communicate with director and editor
Create detailed reports for post-production

Script & Notes: Always has the marked-up script and continuity notes ready

Stopwatch: Times each take to help with editing decisions

Why Continuity Is Critical in Filmmaking

Continuity errors can break the audience's immersion and make a film look unprofessional. Here are real examples of what can go wrong:

Prop Continuity

Glass levels changing between cuts, food appearing/disappearing, objects moving positions

Wardrobe Errors

Ties switching sides, buttons done/undone, jacket on/off, wrinkles changing

Actor Position

Hand positions, head turns, eye lines, sitting/standing inconsistencies

Environmental

Lighting changes, weather inconsistencies, background elements moving

Exercise 1: Lining a Script

Practice Scene

SCENE 15 - COFFEE SHOP - DAY


SARAH sits at a corner table, typing on her laptop. Steam rises from her coffee cup.


SARAH
(looking up from screen)
This deadline is impossible.


MIKE enters, spots Sarah, and walks over.


MIKE
Working hard or hardly working?


Sarah closes the laptop and smiles.


SARAH
You know me too well.

Shot List to Line:
Wide shot: Entire coffee shop
Medium shot: Sarah at table
Close-up: Sarah's face
Over-shoulder: Mike's entrance
Two-shot: Sarah and Mike
Your Task:
  1. 1. Draw colored lines next to the script text
  2. 2. Each line should show which dialogue/action is covered
  3. 3. Use the color coding shown on the left
  4. 4. Lines can overlap when multiple shots cover the same text
  5. 5. Mark any insert shots or cutaways needed

Exercise 2: Spot the Continuity Errors

Study these two "shots" from the same scene and identify the continuity errors:

Shot A (Wide Shot)

  • • Sarah: Blue sweater, coffee cup 3/4 full
  • • Laptop: Open, screen showing document
  • • Table: Napkin under cup, phone to the right
  • • Hair: Pulled back in ponytail
  • • Watch: On left wrist

Shot B (Close-up)

  • • Sarah: Blue sweater, coffee cup 1/2 full
  • • Laptop: Closed
  • • Table: No napkin, phone to the left
  • • Hair: Loose around shoulders
  • • Watch: On right wrist
Errors to Find:

Major Errors:

  • □ Coffee level changed
  • □ Laptop position changed
  • □ Hair continuity broken

Minor Errors:

  • □ Phone moved
  • □ Napkin disappeared
  • □ Watch switched wrists

Practice Tips

• Start with simple scenes before tackling complex action sequences

• Practice timing scenes with a stopwatch - accuracy is crucial

• Take reference photos of everything, even if it seems obvious

• Watch movies with the sound off and look for continuity errors

• Keep detailed notes - memory is unreliable on long shoot days
