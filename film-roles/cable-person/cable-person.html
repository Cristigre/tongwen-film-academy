What is a Cable Person?

A Cable Person (also known as Cable Wrangler) is a specialized crew member responsible for managing, routing, and maintaining all cables on a film set. They work closely with both the Lighting and Camera departments to ensure safe, efficient cable management during production.

Cable laying and routing for camera and lighting equipment
Professional cable coiling and storage
Clearing paths for mobile rigs and dollies

The Over-Under Method

The over-under coiling method is the industry standard for cable management. This technique prevents cable memory and ensures cables lay flat when deployed, extending their lifespan and reducing tangles.

1 Start with the cable connector in your non-dominant hand
2 First loop: coil "over" in a natural clockwise direction
3 Second loop: twist and coil "under" in counter-clockwise direction
4 Continue alternating over-under pattern throughout the cable

Best Practices
  • • Use gaffer tape to secure cables at stress points
  • • Label cables clearly for easy identification
  • • Keep power and signal cables separated
  • • Plan cable runs before equipment setup
  • • Use cable guards in high-traffic areas
  • • Inspect cables regularly for damage
Common Mistakes
  • • Coiling cables in the same direction (creates memory)
  • • Running cables across walkways without protection
  • • Mixing power and signal cables in same conduit
  • • Leaving loose coils that can create trip hazards
  • • Using inadequate strain relief at connections
  • • Ignoring manufacturer bend radius specifications

Safety and Situational Awareness

Dangerous Cable Layouts

  • • Cables running across pedestrian pathways
  • • Loose coils left on the ground
  • • No cable protection in high-traffic areas
  • • Poor visibility of cable runs
  • • Inadequate securing of overhead cables

Safe Cable Management

  • • Cable guards protecting walkways
  • • Proper cable routing along walls
  • • Clear marking of cable paths
  • • Secure overhead cable management
  • • Regular safety inspections

Essential Safety Guidelines

Personal Protection

Always wear appropriate PPE including safety boots and high-visibility clothing when working around cables.

Situational Awareness

Maintain constant awareness of crew movement, equipment changes, and potential hazards throughout the day.

Team Communication

Maintain clear communication with all departments about cable routes and any safety concerns.
