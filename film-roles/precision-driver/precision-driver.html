What Does a Precision Driver Do?

A Precision Driver is a highly specialized professional who executes controlled, repeatable driving maneuvers for film and television productions. Unlike stunt drivers who focus on dramatic crashes and high-speed chases, precision drivers create smooth, choreographed movements that serve the camera and story.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Execute on-camera driving shots with surgical precision
  • Coordinate seamlessly with camera cars and process trailers
  • Rehearse and perfect choreographed vehicle movements
  • Maintain absolute safety for cast, crew, and equipment

Safety Protocols

Safety is the foundation of precision driving. Every maneuver must be executed with zero risk to cast, crew, or equipment while maintaining the creative vision.

Pre-Drive Checklist:

  • • Vehicle mechanical inspection
  • • Route safety assessment
  • • Weather condition evaluation
  • • Emergency equipment check

During Filming:

  • • Constant radio communication
  • • Safety marshals at key positions
  • • Medical team on standby
  • • Clear evacuation procedures

Repeatability Excellence

Directors need multiple takes and angles. Precision drivers must execute identical performances repeatedly, maintaining exact timing, positioning, and vehicle behavior.

Timing Accuracy: ±0.5 seconds
Position Precision: ±6 inches
Speed Consistency: ±2 mph
