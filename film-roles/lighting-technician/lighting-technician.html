What Does a Lighting Technician Do?

Lighting Technicians (also known as Set Electricians or "Sparks") are the skilled professionals responsible for rigging, positioning, and maintaining all lighting equipment on film sets. They work under the guidance of the Gaffer to bring the Director of Photography's vision to life.

Rigging & Setup

Installing and positioning lights, managing power distribution

Cable Management

Running cables safely and efficiently throughout the set

Light Adjustment

Fine-tuning fixtures, hanging gels, adjusting flags and diffusion

Safety Maintenance

Ensuring all equipment meets safety standards and protocols

Supporting the Gaffer and DP

Film Lighting Department Hierarchy

Director of Photography (DP)

Creates the overall visual strategy and lighting design. Communicates the artistic vision that technicians help bring to life through precise equipment placement and adjustment.

Gaffer

Head of the electrical department who translates the DP's vision into technical reality. Directly supervises lighting technicians and coordinates all electrical operations on set.

Lighting Technician

The hands-on specialists who execute the lighting plan. They rig equipment, manage power, make adjustments, and ensure every technical detail supports the creative vision.

Lighting Tools and Rigging Techniques

Essential Equipment

Lighting Fixtures

Fresnel lights, LED panels, tungsten units, and specialty fixtures for various shooting conditions.

Rigging Hardware

C-stands, baby stands, grip arms, clamps, and mounting accessories for secure light positioning.

Light Modifiers

Gels, diffusion materials, flags, nets, and bounce boards to shape and control light quality.

Power & Distribution

Extension cables, distro boxes, dimmers, and power management systems for safe electricity distribution.

Basic Rigging Principles

Secure Mounting

Always ensure lights are properly secured with appropriate stands and safety cables to prevent accidents.

Cable Management

Run cables along walls and tape them down to prevent tripping hazards and equipment damage.

Weight Distribution

Balance heavy fixtures properly and use sandbags or counterweights when necessary for stability.

Safety First on Set

Critical Safety Reminder

Lighting technicians work with high-voltage electricity, heavy equipment, and work at heights. Safety protocols are not optional – they save lives and prevent injuries.

Essential Safety Equipment

Hard hat for overhead work
Safety glasses/goggles
Work gloves for handling equipment
Steel-toed boots
Fall protection harness for height work

Safety Protocols

1

Always turn off power before making electrical connections

2

Test equipment before each use and inspect for damage

3

Use proper lifting techniques for heavy equipment

4

Maintain clear communication during rigging operations

5

Keep fire extinguishers accessible and know their locations
