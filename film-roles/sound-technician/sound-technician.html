What Does a Sound Technician Do?

Sound Technicians (also called Utility Sound Technicians) are the backbone of any professional sound department. They work behind the scenes to ensure that every piece of audio equipment functions flawlessly and every sound is captured with pristine quality.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Cable setup and management
  • Wireless microphone preparation and placement
  • Equipment testing and troubleshooting
  • Supporting boom operators during complex shots
  • Antenna positioning and RF coordination

A professional sound cart - the mobile command center for audio capture

Supporting the Sound Mixer and Boom Operators

Working with the Sound Mixer

  • Prepare and test all equipment before each scene
  • Monitor wireless frequencies and signal strength
  • Manage backup systems and redundancy plans
  • Handle equipment logistics and transportation

Assisting Boom Operators

  • Manage boom pole cables and prevent tangling
  • Provide backup boom poles and microphones
  • Assist with mic placement on actors
  • Help navigate complex blocking and camera moves

Sound Technicians work closely with boom operators to ensure seamless audio capture

On-Set Problem Solving

Common Challenges

RF Interference

Cell towers, other wireless devices, or production equipment causing signal issues

Cable Failures

Damaged connectors, shorts, or tangled cables during complex scenes

Battery Drain

Unexpected power loss in wireless transmitters during critical takes

Last-Minute Changes

Script changes requiring rapid equipment reconfiguration

Quick Solutions

Frequency Agility

Always have backup frequencies scanned and ready to deploy instantly

Cable Organization

Pre-labeled, tested backup cables easily accessible from the sound cart

Power Management

Standardized battery rotation system with charge level monitoring

Adaptability

Stay calm, communicate clearly, and always have Plan B (and C) ready
