Understanding the Film Hair Stylist Role

A Hair Stylist in film production is not your typical salon professional. They are specialized craftspeople who work as integral members of the film crew, responsible for creating and maintaining character-specific hairstyles that serve the story, maintain visual continuity, and collaborate seamlessly with other departments.

Character Development

Creating hairstyles that reflect character personality, social status, and story arc

Technical Precision

Maintaining frame-by-frame continuity across multiple shooting days

Team Collaboration

Working closely with makeup, wardrobe, and camera departments

What Does a Hair Stylist Do on a Film Set?

Professional Hair Stylist at work on a film production set

1

Script Analysis & Character Research

Breaking down the script to understand each character's journey, social background, and the time period of the story to inform hairstyle choices.

2

Pre-Production Design

Creating mood boards, conducting hair tests with actors, and coordinating with other departments to establish the visual language.

3

On-Set Execution

Styling actors before scenes, maintaining continuity between takes, and making quick adjustments as needed during filming.

4

Specialized Techniques

Working with wigs, extensions, period-accurate styling, and special effects hair applications for unique character requirements.

Key Insight

Unlike salon stylists who focus on making clients look their best, film hair stylists serve the story first. They might intentionally create "bad" hair days, aging effects, or historically accurate styles that prioritize character authenticity over contemporary beauty standards.

Continuity in Hair Styling: Frame-by-Frame Accuracy

The technical precision required to maintain consistent hair appearance across multiple shooting days and scenes

The Continuity Challenge

Films are rarely shot in chronological order. A hair stylist must ensure that a character's hair looks identical whether it's shot on day 1 or day 50 of production. This requires meticulous documentation and precise recreation techniques.

Detailed photo documentation from multiple angles
Comprehensive notes on products and techniques used
Time-stamped records for each scene and take

Professional continuity sheet used for tracking hair and makeup details
